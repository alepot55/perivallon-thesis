### Slide 4 — What a satellite actually measures
**Titolo:** *A satellite image is a spectral cube, not a photograph*

**Contenuto:**
- Each pixel = vector of reflectance values
- RGB = 3 broad visible bands (colour only)
- Multispectral (MS) = 4-15 chosen bands. SuperDove records 8.
- Hyperspectral (HSI) = 100s of contiguous narrow bands
- This vector is the material's **spectral signature** — input to any classifier

**Refs:** `kokaly-2017-splib07a` (USGS) + Planet specs

**Visual:** diagramma stack di bande + cubo iperspettrale stylized

### Slide 5 — Every material has a spectral fingerprint
**Titolo:** *Every material has a spectral fingerprint*

**Contenuto:**
- Reflectance curves diagnose material chemistry, not just colour
- Diagnostic bands per material family:
  - Vegetation: NIR plateau (chlorophyll Red drop @0.68 µm)
  - Plastic / hydrocarbons: C-H @ 1.66 / 1.73 µm (SWIR)
  - Asbestos-cement: Mg-OH @ 2.31 µm (SWIR)
  - Soil/clay: Al-OH @ 2.20 µm (SWIR)

**Refs:** `kokaly-2017-splib07a` · `cilia-2015-ac-weathering` · `zhou-2021-plastic-classifier`

**Visual:** plot riflettanze 4 materiali singoli (vegetazione, suolo, cemento, asbestos). **Scene single-material**, niente mix. Generato io da USGS splib07a convoluzioni S2/WV-3.

### Slide 6 — RGB fails in two distinct ways
**Titolo:** *RGB fails in two distinct ways*

**Contenuto** (snellito vs attuale):
- **Iso-chromaticity**: same colour, different materials (e.g. concrete vs asbestos-cement vs limestone)
- **Sub-pixel mixing**: different materials average into the same chromatic signature
- Both failures vanish in SWIR — features become chemistry, not albedo

**Refs:** `zhou-2021-plastic-classifier` · `aguilar-2025-macroplastics-wv3` · `kokaly-2017-splib07a`

**Visual:** 2 esempi affiancati (foto materiale 1 vs materiale 2, stesso colore in RGB, diverse in SWIR)

### Slide 7 — Sensor landscape (RADAR CHART, riformulato + spostato qui)
**Titolo:** *The sensor trade-off: spatial × spectral × revisit*

**Contenuto:**
- 3 axes (radar/triangle chart): spatial GSD, # spectral bands, revisit time

**Refs:** sensor specs (Planet, ESA, USGS, Maxar) + `reference_sensor_tradeoffs.md`

### Slide 13 — More bands ≠ more information (snellito)
**Titolo:** *More bands is not always more — but the right ones help*

**Contenuto** (snellito):
- Few well-chosen bands can rival hyperspectral on material discrimination
- Measured evidence:
  - `aguilar-2021-wv3-ablation`: VNIR 90.85% → SWIR alone 96.79% (NDPI dominates)

- *Caveat*: most evidence is SWIR-based. VNIR-only is the open question of this thesis.

### Slide 14 — SuperDove: the chosen trade-off
**Titolo:** *SuperDove: the chosen trade-off for this thesis*

**Contenuto:**

- 8 VNIR bands (Coastal Blue, Blue, 2×Green, Yellow, Red, Red Edge, NIR)
- Sub-daily revisit, **free for research**
- Radiometry validated against S-2 (mean error ~6%)
- *Known constraint*: no SWIR

### Slide 16 — DOFA: a sensor-agnostic backbone (slide dedicata, richiesta Thomas)
**Titolo:** *DOFA: a band-agnostic backbone — candidate to start from*

**Contenuto:**
- **Dynamic One-For-All** (Xiong et al. 2024): hypernetwork generates patch-embedding weights conditioned on each input band's central wavelength
- → One backbone handles S-2 (13b), SuperDove (8b), WV-3 (8/16b), or HSI
- → Generalises to unseen sensor configurations at inference
- → Natural fit for the band-ablation study (RGB / +NIR / +RedEdge / full 8b)

### Slide 17 — The closest competitor (Shepherd 2025)
**Titolo:** *The closest competitor: spaceborne hyperspectral asbestos detection*

**Contenuto:**
